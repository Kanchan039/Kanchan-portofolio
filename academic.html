Research Interests

My primary interest lies in mathematical analysis, with a focus on rigorous proof techniques and conceptual clarity. I have a strong liking for topology and its abstract mathematical thinking, developed during my M.Sc. at IIT Kanpur. My project experience includes homology in algebraic topology and a reading project in multivariable calculus, which strengthened my analytical skills and geometric intuition. At this stage, I am dedicated to building a strong foundation in analysis and topology and developing as a proof-oriented thinker, while remaining open to discovering new directions in mathematics.

Major Projects

  • Conceptual Foundations in Multivariate Calculus — Reading project focusing on strengthening conceptual clarity, geometric intuition, and rigorous proofs, under the supervision of Prof. Sivaguru R., TIFR CAM, Bangalore (Jun 2025–Aug 2025).
  • Equivalence of Simplicial and Singular Homology in Delta Complex — Studied algebraic invariants of topological spaces, established the equivalence of simplicial and singular homology, and applied the theory to results like the Brouwer Fixed Point Theorem. Project was under the supervision of Dr. Mohit Upmanyu (Post-Doc at CMI) at Ashoka University, Haryana.
