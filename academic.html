Research Interests

My primary interest lies in mathematical analysis, particularly in areas that emphasize rigorous proof techniques and conceptual clarity. I have especially enjoyed working with real analysis and several variable calculus, and I find deep satisfaction in understanding and constructing precise mathematical arguments. In my MSc journey at IIT Kanpur, I developed a strong liking for topology, which I studied in my second semester. This exposure sparked my interest in abstract mathematical thinking. During the summer of 2025, I pursued two projects — one based on homology, which gave me a preliminary glimpse into algebraic topology, and another, a reading project in ,multivariable calculusstrong>, which strengthened my analytical skills and geometric intuition. At this stage, I am eager to further explore analysis, deepen my understanding of topology, and continue developing as a proof-oriented thinker. While I remain open to discovering new directions in the future, my current focus is on building a strong foundation in analysis and exploring how mathematical ideas are developed and justified through precise logical reasoning.
